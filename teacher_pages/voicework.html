About My Work

Body ~ Voice ~ Emotions
I provide a safe and supportive, non-judgemental environment where my students, complete beginners to professional singers, are always encouraged to enjoy their own gift of singing and discover their full potential. I encourage my students to expand their emotional repertoire, engage their heart and their passion for singing, and experience vocal freedom.

Through my own experience of hearing myself sing and listening to others, I discovered that the real beauty of a sound only emerges when it is sung from the heart. Your voice and emotions are intrinsically linked to who you are. I use voice work for personal as well as creative and artistic growth.

Often I hear people say that they only sing in their bathroom. What stops us from singing with the same uninhibited freedom we were blessed with as children?

My aim is to bring back the freedom, the joy of singing and the feeling of being alive.

I compare voice work to the rough piece of material that a sculptor has front of him before starting his work. The difference with the voice is that you don’t see it, only hear it. You have to let the ‘raw material’ out in order to gain control. The human voice is trained with the piano but singing is not just hitting the right notes, sounds are an integral part of vocal ability. In a safe environment you can allow yourself to explore your full vocal potential and learn to simultaneously create freedom while maintaining control.

• Singing is physical
“The sound has two directions ~ I am singing down through my body and then into the space.” Patricia BARDI (New York)
All of your body is your instrument. It is important to nourish the relationship between the voice and body to embody the voice. Movement facilitates body awareness, physical tension release, focus, balance with the thoughts, voice projection and vocal freedom.

• Singing is emotional
Reach in ~ Reach out
Singing is a powerful energy. A unique tool which helps the singer to reach inside their heart and release unexpressed emotions; through vocal sounds, melodies and lyrics.
On a personal level you can explore your emotions and discover yourself, through working with the voice. On a creative level you will become a more dynamic and expressive singer by using your inner feelings creatively and singing words with true meaning.

About Myself
I started teaching in the early 1990’s in response to requests from members of the audience after my concerts. “How do you sing so beautifully?” They asked with amazement in their eyes, and so my experiences with teaching people how to sing began. My work expanded as I realised that singing and voice work helps people to feel better in themselves and feel empowered. My combination of voice work and healing developed naturally as singing is instinctively connected to the human soul. I continue to develop my work as I never stop learning about the voice – the unique tool for the expression of human emotions.
I have a City & Guilds Certificate in Further Education Teaching from The City Literary Institute in London.
