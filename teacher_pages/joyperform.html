Weekend workshop

    

What matters in performance is not what you do…
But how you do it…
If you believe in what you do… the audience will believe it too

    


What is the workshop about?
This workshop is designed to give you the tools you need to reach your full potential as a performer. The opportunity to perform in a venue environment with a professional pianist is part of this workshop.

 

SELF-CONFIDENCE - PRESENCE - PROFESSIONAL ATTITUDE - OVERCOMING STAGE FRIGHT- EXPRESSION - BODY LANGAGE - MOVEMENT- COMMUNICATION WITH AUDIENCE - MICROPHONE TECHNIQUE - THE JOY OF PERFORMING - PREPARATION WORK BEFORE PERFORMANCE

    

Performance is visual - whatever you do is seen and looked at. You need to look confident, be focused and sound good. We will concentrate on physical awareness and expression. Learn to differentiate body language from movement and experiment on a practical level. The aim of this workshop is to make you aware of what you do with your body whilst on stage (what do you do with your hands, your eyes, your feet and your whole body). Once you are aware of your body language, you can incorporate it and use it in your performance.

Having a good voice is not enough! You need to make the song yours and touch your audience. Mean what you sing, learn to deliver your message and communicate with conviction, belief and feelings. The basis of this workshop is developing the spirit and gift of performance. This is not a technical course, but I will refer to vocal technique as is necessary.

Who is this workshop for?
Anyone who is motivated to learn about performance through to semi professional and professional singers who want to improve their performance skills. Some singing experience is required.

What do you need to bring?
Bring a song that you know by heart. Be prepared to perform from the first day of the workshop. You are welcome to bring your own backing track or your own instrument if you wish to accompany yourself. Microphones and amplification will be provided. You need to have the sheet music and lyric of the song you want to perform.

Wear loose and comfortable clothing.
