How can I describe what I am doing now without telling you where I have come from? I started my career in 1980 in Martinique singing in a French Caribbean style. It was one of the best times of my life! Originally I was going to stay on the island for 3 months but I ended up staying much longer. Infact that was where my first concert and recordings took place with the committed and dedicated band Kanpech. It was a totally magical experience!

With singer Monette Duval, Kanpech -
Martinique 1980

Since then I have had many incredible and unique experiences.

I returned to France and worked as a session singer performing, touring and recording with unlimited French Caribbean African artists.

I was progressively moving into the world of Jazz. By 1985 I was studying the trumpet at the CIM in Paris. One of my most unforgettable influences was Lester Bowie. My love for jazz was complete.

In London in 1983 Free improvised Music had taken root inspired by the band Dreamtime. This led me to move to London and by 1987 I was in full pursuit, immersed in free style.

During my experience with the London improvising scene I came across an enormous number of musicians and artists singing with every combination I could possibly have dreamt of, from duet to large ensemble and big band. One of my favourite combinations was with voice and double bass.

An amazing partnership ensued with pianist Alex Maguire, also with Steve Beresford singing as a duet with his ensemble and with the Path Thomas Trio.

Singing with my dearest friend Maggie Nicols will always be a memorable time in my life as was my first Jazz festival in 1988 with the band Fonolite.
